# Non-commodity anchoring (do this before drafting)

Generic AI content doesn't get read or cited. Before writing, extract anchors
only the company could provide. If you can't find them, interview the user.

## The 4 anchors
1. **The concrete thing** — a specific instance, customer, or event (not a category).
2. **The counter-intuitive claim** — what the company believes that the obvious
   take gets wrong.
3. **The proof artifact** — a real number, screenshot, result, or named case.
4. **The generalizable lesson** — what the reader can take away and apply.

## Interview prompts (if an anchor is missing)
- "What's a specific time this happened — a real customer, a real result?"
- "What does everyone in your space get wrong about this?"
- "What's one number or outcome you can stand behind publicly?"

## Hard rule
Never invent a client, a number, a quote, or a result. If no proof exists,
either get it from the user or cut the claim. An honest, narrower piece beats a
fabricated comprehensive one.

## Headline approaches (pick by anchor)
- number-led ("We cut X by N%") — when you have a proof number
- refusal-led ("Why we stopped doing X") — when you have the counter-claim
- surprise-led ("The thing nobody tells you about X") — when you have the lesson
